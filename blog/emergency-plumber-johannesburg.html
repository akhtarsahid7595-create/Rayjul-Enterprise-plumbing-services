What Counts as a Plumbing Emergency in Johannesburg?

Not every plumbing issue is an emergency — but these situations require immediate professional attention to prevent serious property damage or health risks:

💥

Burst Pipe

A burst pipe can flood your home in minutes. Turn off the main water supply immediately and call us.

🔥

Burst Geyser

Burst geysers cause ceiling and wall damage fast. Turn off the cold water inlet and call Drain Doctor now.

🚽

Sewage Backup

Sewage overflow is a serious health hazard. A blocked main sewer line requires immediate clearing.

🌊

Indoor Flooding

Water flooding from any plumbing source — turn off the main stopcock and call us immediately.

❄️

No Hot Water

No hot water, especially with a geyser that was working, often signals geyser failure or element burnout.

💧

Water Leak in Wall

Water stains spreading on walls or ceiling indicate a hidden pipe leak causing structural damage. You need specialised leak detection to find it fast.

What to Do Before the Emergency Plumber Arrives in Johannesburg

While waiting for Drain Doctor to arrive, these steps can limit damage to your Johannesburg home:

1

Turn Off the Main Water Supply

Locate your main stopcock (usually near the water meter at the property boundary in Gauteng) and turn it clockwise to shut off the water supply to your home.

2

Turn Off the Geyser (if relevant)

If your geyser has burst or is damaged, switch it off at the DB (distribution board) to prevent the heating element burning out in an empty tank. This also reduces electricity risk.

3

Move Valuables Away from Flooding Water

Move furniture, electronics, and important documents away from affected areas to prevent secondary damage.

4

Document the Damage for Insurance

Take photos and video of the damage before any cleanup — this is essential for insurance claims in South Africa. Note: your insurer may require a plumbing COC certificate for the claim.

5
