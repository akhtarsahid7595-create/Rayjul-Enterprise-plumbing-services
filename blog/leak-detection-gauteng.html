Common Signs You Have a Hidden Leak

Many leaks in Johannesburg homes happen underground or inside walls where they aren't immediately visible. Look out for these "silent" warning signs:

📈 Skyrocketing Water Bills

If your bill has jumped significantly without a change in usage, it's the #1 indicator of a continuous leak.

🌀 Spinning Water Meter

Turn off all taps. If the little dial on your water meter is still spinning, water is escaping somewhere in your system.

🍄 Damp Patches & Mold

Discolored spots on ceilings, peeling paint, or a musty smell are clear signs of water ingress behind surfaces.

Why DIY Leak Detection Rarely Works

While you can check for a leaking toilet or a dripping tap, 90% of costly leaks are slab leaks (under your foundation) or main line leaks (between the meter and your house). Attempting to find these by digging up your yard or breaking walls based on a "guess" leads to massive unnecessary costs.

Professional leak detection in Gauteng involves non-invasive technology:

  • Acoustic Detection: Ultra-sensitive microphones that "hear" the sound of pressurized water escaping.
  • Thermal Imaging: Identifying temperature differences in walls or floors that signal moisture.
  • Tracer Gas: Inserting a safe, specialized gas into the pipes and detecting where it escapes.

What to Do if You Suspect a Leak

Don't wait. The longer you wait, the more water you pay for and the more damage your property sustains. If you are in Johannesburg, Pretoria, or anywhere in Gauteng, follow these steps:

  1. Isolate the House: Close the main stopcock at the street. If the meter stops spinning, the leak is likely inside or under the house.
  2. Check the Geyser: A leaking geyser often overflows through the pressure valve. Check your overflow pipes.
  3. Call a Specialist: Contact Drain Doctor. We specialize in leak detection and repair across Gauteng.
