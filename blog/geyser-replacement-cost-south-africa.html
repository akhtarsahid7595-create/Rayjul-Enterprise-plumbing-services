Repair vs. Replace — Which is Better for Your South African Geyser?

Not every geyser problem requires a full replacement. Here's how to decide:

Repair (if your geyser is under 7–8 years old)

These issues can usually be repaired cost-effectively:

  • Failed heating element — R800 to R1,500 to replace
  • Faulty thermostat — R600 to R1,200 to replace
  • Pressure control valve (PCV) failure — R500 to R1,000
  • Thermostat scald guard failure — R400 to R900

Replace (if your geyser is over 8–10 years old)

  • Leaking from the tank body — indicates internal corrosion, replacement required
  • Repeated element failures — often caused by excessive scale build-up in hard water areas
  • Geyser older than 10 years — replacement more cost-effective than ongoing repairs
  • Burst or cracked tank — emergency replacement required immediately

Frequently Asked Questions — Geyser Replacement South Africa

How much does a geyser replacement cost in South Africa in 2025?
A full geyser replacement in South Africa in 2025 costs R4,500 to R12,000 for a standard electric geyser, fully installed including the COC certificate. A 150L Kwikot or Ariston geyser fully installed in Gauteng typically costs R6,000 to R9,500.
How long does a geyser last in South Africa?
A standard electric geyser in South Africa lasts 8 to 12 years. Hard water areas in Gauteng (like parts of the East Rand) can shorten lifespan due to scale. Installing a Geyserwise controller and geyser blanket can extend the lifespan significantly.
Can my home insurance cover a burst geyser in South Africa?
Most South African home insurance policies cover burst geysers as an insured event. However, the insurer may require a valid COC certificate for the installation. Always report the burst geyser to your insurer immediately and take photos of the damage.
