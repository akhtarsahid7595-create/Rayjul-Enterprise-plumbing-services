Blocked Drain in Johannesburg? Causes, DIY Tips & When to Call a Plumber

A blocked drain in Johannesburg is one of the most common plumbing problems faced by Gauteng homeowners. Whether it's a slow kitchen drain, a blocked toilet in Pretoria, or a backed-up storm water drain in Sandton — this guide will help you understand the causes, what you can do yourself, and when it's time to call a professional plumber in Gauteng.

Why Do Drains Block in Johannesburg Homes?

Understanding the causes of blocked drains in Gauteng helps you prevent them from happening again. Here are the most common culprits:

  • 🍳 Grease & fat build-up — The #1 cause of kitchen drain blockages in Johannesburg. Cooking oil solidifies in pipes over time.
  • 🌳 Tree root ingress — Common in older Gauteng suburbs. Tree roots seek out moisture and crack sewer pipes, causing serious blockages.
  • 🚿 Soap scum & hair — Bathroom drains accumulate soap residue and hair, creating stubborn clogs over time.
  • 🚽 Foreign objects — Wet wipes, cotton buds, and sanitary products flushed down toilets are a major cause of blocked drains and toilets.
  • 🏗️ Collapsed or cracked pipes — Common in Gauteng properties with older clay or cast-iron pipes, especially in areas with clay-heavy soil.
  • 🌧️ Storm water overflow — Heavy Gauteng summer rains can overwhelm storm water drains, especially if they're partially blocked with debris.
💡 Johannesburg-specific tip: The clay subsoil problem

Many Johannesburg suburbs sit on expansive clay soils that shift significantly during summer rain and winter drought cycles. This constant ground movement puts stress on buried drain pipes, making cracked and displaced pipes more common in Gauteng than in other South African provinces. If you experience recurring blockages, a specialised leak detection and CCTV inspection is recommended.

Signs You Have a Blocked Drain in Your Gauteng Home

  • Water draining slowly from sinks, baths, or showers
  • Gurgling sounds coming from drains or toilets
  • Unpleasant sewage smell from drains or outside gullies
  • Water backing up into other fixtures (e.g., toilet bubbles when you use the sink)
  • Puddles in the garden or yard near sewer inspection covers
  • Flooding in the toilet or bathroom when flushing
⚠️ Multiple blocked drains = call a plumber immediately

If more than one drain in your home is blocked simultaneously (e.g., toilet and kitchen sink), this indicates a blockage in your main sewer line — not just a surface drain. This requires immediate professional attention from a plumber and should not be ignored.

DIY Methods to Unblock a Drain in South Africa

For minor, surface-level blockages in Johannesburg homes, these DIY methods may help:

1

Boiling Water

Pour a full kettle of boiling water slowly down the blocked drain. Effective for grease and soap blockages in kitchen sinks. Repeat 2–3 times if needed. Note: Do not use on PVC pipes — use hot (not boiling) water instead.

2

Baking Soda & Vinegar

Pour ½ cup of baking soda (bicarbonate of soda) into the drain, followed by ½ cup of white vinegar. Cover and wait 30 minutes, then flush with hot water. Good for mild organic blockages.

3

Plunger

Use a cup plunger for sink blockages, or a flange plunger for toilets. Create a tight seal over the drain and plunge vigorously 10–15 times. Effective for fresh blockages close to the drain opening.

4

Drain Snake / Auger

A manual or electric drain snake (available from hardware stores like Builders Warehouse or Leroy Merlin in Gauteng) can break up blockages 1–3 metres into the pipe. Good for hair clogs in bathroom drains.

When to Call a Professional Plumber in Gauteng for Blocked Drains

Stop the DIY and call a qualified plumber in Johannesburg or Gauteng if:

  • DIY methods haven't worked after 2–3 attempts
  • Multiple drains are blocked at the same time
  • You can smell sewage inside or outside your home
  • Water is backing up from the floor drain or toilet
  • You suspect tree root ingress or a collapsed pipe
  • Your storm water drain keeps overflowing during rain
